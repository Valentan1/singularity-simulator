# Singularity Simulator

## Overview
The Singularity Simulator is a modeling engine designed to visualize and test systems approaching infinite runaway metrics versus structural capacity limits. It works in tandem with the VTCS framework to project how data behaves when forced to confront a mathematical or physical plateau.

## Why it was Built
Many standard physical models assume infinite linear expansion. When observational data begins to spike or accelerate, standard models often predict a "singularity" or a systemic break. This simulator tests the alternative: what happens when that acceleration is actually just the steep curve of a logistic function approaching its saturation point?

## Key Features
* **Runaway vs. Bound Simulation:** Compare standard infinite-growth projections against capacity-bound logistic models.
* **Stress-Testing Datasets:** Extrapolate short-term data to test long-term stability and identify where traditional models break.
* **Visualizing the Plateau:** See exactly where a system is forced to slow down due to structural or physical limits.

## Integration with VTCS
While the **VTCS Microscope** is used for diagnosing past and present data, the **Singularity Simulator** is used for projecting the future state of those systems. Together, they prove that systems do not break; they reach capacity.
